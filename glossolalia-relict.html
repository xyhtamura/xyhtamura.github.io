glossolalia: relict

a tongue that has outlived its speakers

plant one ancestor on the IPA tray. it breeds forward without you — each generation inherits its parent’s vowel, then drifts, the whole lineage walking across the vowel space like a sound-law in progress. where drift piles up, a population splits, and a new consonant crystallises at the break. the dead leave hairline trails. the page reconstructs a proto‑form from whoever still speaks.
