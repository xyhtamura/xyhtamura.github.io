Xyh Tamura is an interdisciplinary/transdisciplinary artist, singer-songwriter, and researcher engaging with posthumanities, techno-global ontologies, and vernacular cultures. They are a graduate of Physics (Theoretical subgroup) from the University of the Philippines and are currently taking a masters in Creative Writing.

Currently Xyh's work involves experimental pop, intermedia and conceptual art, videopoetry, and electronic literature leveraging digital environments and multisensory experiences, often from a personal, Philippine (Ilonggo) and Japanese perspective and incorporating the sciences. They are researching the perception of tuning systems across cultures and developing post-MIDI technologies to support non-standard tuning systems in music. They are also doing research on how technology and globalization shape ontologies, and how global, subcultural, and vernacular life-worlds evolve alongside these changes.
