Xyh Tamura does transdisciplinary art, performance, and research colliding music, writing, visuals, and interaction, exploring intermedia poetics, posthumanities, and the intersections of science, technology, culture, and globalization. They hold a degree in Physics (Theoretical subgroup) from the University of the Philippines Diliman and are currently pursuing a Master’s in Creative Writing (Poetry). Xyh utilizes voice, keyboards, and electronics as instruments.

Their work involves experimental songwriting, electronic literature, videopoetry, conceptual and generative art, and participatory approaches, incorporating science, digital environments, and multimedia experiences often approached from an intersectional, Filipino (Ilonggo) and Japanese perspective. Xyh has developed web-based tools for image manipulation and audio visualization and is working in music, from composing film scores, audio engineering, to being a session musician. They do research in philosophy, social development, and science. They also do work in graphic design, fashion, and virtual worldbuilding.
