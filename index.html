Xyh Tamura does interdisciplinary/transdisciplinary art, performance, and research working across music, writing, art, and interaction, exploring intermedia poetics, posthumanities, and the intersections of science, technology, culture, and globalization. They hold a degree in Physics (Theoretical subgroup) from the University of the Philippines and are currently pursuing a Master’s in Creative Writing (Poetry).

Xyh’s current practice involves experimental songwriting, electronic literature, videopoetry, and conceptual art, often incorporating science, digital environments, and multimodal experiences from an intersectional Filipino (Ilonggo) and Japanese perspective. They are developing post-MIDI solutions to support non-standard tuning systems in music and have created a web-based tool for digital image manipulation. Xyh also does research in philosophy and social development, composes scores for film and video, produces, mixes, and masters music and audio, does graphic design, and is involved in makeup and fashion.
