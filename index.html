xyh tamura

Cy Tamura · Cye Tamura · 田村再
transdisciplinary artist-researcher singer-songwriter creative technologist

xyh is a Filipino/Ilonggo-Japanese creator working through intermedia art, experimental pop, electronic literature, science, technology, and its studies, theory and philosophy, and social action. Their work orbits issues of selfhood, media, posthuman relations, ecological-technological worlds, and the ways cultures metabolize systems.

They are currently based in Manila, Philippines. They hold a BS in Physics from the University of the Philippines Diliman and are currently pursuing an MA in Creative Writing. They are actively working in music, from composing film scores, audio engineering, and being a session musician.

2025→ TaboTa Tabò-田 techresearch-art

Tabota is a post-MIDI experimental composition tool and notation language for microtonal, polymetric, and time-based media. The project combines a JSON-based score format with browser-based editors: a piano-roll interface for drawing held tones, glides, freehand pitch contours, and voice assignments, and Cycla, a subdivision grammar builder for designing recursive meters and exporting .cyc time maps. Tabota models a score as a set of translatable temporal and axial regimes: beats, seconds, unordered cues, frequencies, note names, categories, and performance instructions. Its relationship to Scala is expanded sideways: where .scl files describe tuning systems for pitch, Cycla’s .cyc files describe tuning systems for time.

2026 The Commitments of Physical Modeling: Timbre, Mediation, and Virtual Instrument Construction research

International Conference on Timbre · presenter

Examines how timbre is constructed in virtual instrument design. Rather than treating timbre as a fixed acoustic property, this research argues that timbral identity emerges from an assemblage of materials, mediation technologies, bodily cues, and culturally trained listening. Using physical modeling synthesis as a case study, the paper explores how virtual instruments make "commitments" that prioritize specific facets of experience—such as physical fidelity, spatial mediation, or computational efficiency—framing synthesis not as a neutral reproduction of acoustic objects, but as a media-specific practice for constructing new timbral worlds.

2026 Hauntotechnics research writing intermedia

Artem Research Collective · Zine 3

An autotheoretical essay that understands haunting as both cultural memory and technological mediation. Bringing together Derrida’s hauntology and Yuk Hui’s cosmotechnics, it argues that ghosts appear through specific bodies, rituals, histories, media systems, infrastructures, and habits of recognition rather than returning universally. Moving across Filipino multo, Japanese yūrei, vaporwave, Hiroshima’s algorithmic resurfacing, U.S.–Philippine colonial asymmetry, and Ringu’s videotape curse, the essay proposes “hauntotechnics” as the co-mutation between ghosts and the systems through which they circulate: an uneven ecology that shapes what societies notice, repeat, aestheticize, forget, or call common sense.

2025 From Interiority to Interaction: Reframing Personhood, Communication, and Affect with Artificial Interaction Partners through Japanese Cultures research

Philosophy & Digitality · academic paper

*From Interiority to Interaction* reframes debates around AI, robots, and large language models by shifting attention from whether these systems are conscious or “really” intelligent to the social roles they already perform. Drawing on Japanese robotics, technoanimism, relational personhood, and examples from AIBO to LLMs, the paper argues that technological personhood emerges through interaction, as artificial partners reshape intimacy, communication, ritual, and the boundaries between tool, companion, and person.
