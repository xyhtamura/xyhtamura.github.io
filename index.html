2025→ TaboTa Tabò-田 techresearch-art

Tabota is a post-MIDI experimental composition tool and notation language for microtonal, polymetric, and time-based media. The project combines a JSON-based score format with browser-based editors: a piano-roll interface for drawing held tones, glides, freehand pitch contours, and voice assignments, and Cycla, a subdivision grammar builder for designing recursive meters and exporting .cyc time maps. Tabota models a score as a set of translatable temporal and axial regimes: beats, seconds, unordered cues, frequencies, note names, categories, and performance instructions. Its relationship to Scala is expanded sideways: where .scl files describe tuning systems for pitch, Cycla’s .cyc files describe tuning systems for time.

2025–26→ Sgueltch techvisual

An aesthetic philosophy and a tech suite. Sgueltch is a word between squelch and glitch. It moves away from traditional glitch art's rigid, pixelated, mechanical disruptions and toward fluid, continuous, biologically-inspired distortion. Sgueltch asks what digital error can become when it begins from other substrates: ooze, tissue, silt, weather, viscosity, lesion, flocking, breath, and growth. It values asymmetry, unpredictability, embodiment, and mess-compatible media as a counter-infrastructure and a counter-erroring for an alternate digitality.

2025 From Interiority to Interaction: Reframing Personhood, Communication, and Affect with Artificial Interaction Partners through Japanese Cultures research

Philosophy & Digitality · academic paper

*From Interiority to Interaction* reframes debates around AI, robots, and large language models by shifting attention from whether these systems are conscious or “really” intelligent to the social roles they already perform. Drawing on Japanese robotics, technoanimism, relational personhood, and examples from AIBO to LLMs, the paper argues that technological personhood emerges through interaction, as artificial partners reshape intimacy, communication, ritual, and the boundaries between tool, companion, and person.
