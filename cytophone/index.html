cytophone

Seven irregular playable body generators: cells, fibers, ghosts, pores, flocks, boundary layers, and impossible stones. Each cytophone turns a small 2D ecology into sound through gesture, physics, modal synthesis, pressure, scrape, breath, and drift. Play alone or as a messy ensemble.

↗
understory
low · struck · 58–360 hz
Membrane-gongs using a drumhead spectrum. Bronze, inharmonic, long-decaying.
 ↗
halocline
mid · bowed · 140–520 hz
A sustained saline boundary layer. Held tones swell, beat, and pull against each other.
 ↗
xenolith
high · struck · 78–1020 hz
Modal synthesis, bell-glass clangor, and hybrid materials.
 ↗
cambium
fiber · plucked · friction/reed
Interconnected cells that can be plucked. Connected cells ring on their own.
 ↗
ectoplasm
motor · scraped · nudge clock
Draggable ghost motors that twitch. Scrapes, subharmonic groans, and crackles.
 ↗
murmuration
flock · chirp · scatter
A cloud of small chirpy percussives.
 ↗
stomata
pore · pressure · breath noise
Breathing noise puff apertures. Press to build pressure, drag to open, and release spore-like puffs.
